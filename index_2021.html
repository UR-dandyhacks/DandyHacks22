About

Meliora!

DandyHacks grows ever better, as we enter our 9th year as the University of Rochester’s annual hackathon; recovering from the pandemic, we have returned to an in person event! Being a fully student-led organization at a tier 1 research university, we try to embody our school’s values as we seek to encourage participants to question the world and learn more.

Growing with us!

For everyone, courage is required to enter competitions. At DandyHacks, we aim to provide a comfortable and friendly environment where courageous students are able to fully absorb themselves in the competition. We hope that regardless of losing or winning the competition, students will have the opportunity to be brave, share the same interest in coding with others, and have fun.

TRACKS

Track #1

Video Games

The video game track is geared towards projects that may focus on either enhancing the experience of playing video games, or projects that are video games themselves.

Track #2

Community

The community track concerns projects involved with doing good for the community. This can include addressing topics such as diversity, equity, inclusion, community service, etc. with a creative and unique perspective. Any project that makes communities stronger and better connected qualifies for this track.

Track #3

Sustainability

One of the most pressing issues in our generation is how we can change our mindset, habits, and daily life to be more sustainable. Through education, accessibility, and creating new technologies to be more green, projects in this track aim to improve our chances of a better future.

FAQ

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event in which people meet to collaboratively build out their ideas using technology!

Who is eligible to join?

Any graduate or undergraduate students over the age of 18 enrolled in a North American institution is eligible.

All hackers must be vaccinated, we will require proof upon check-in.

Everyone must be masked indoors according to university policy.

Do I have to pay to get in?

We will never ask you to pay a single dollar. DandyHacks is completely free. We will foot the bill for all the speakers, food, and swag.

Will the hackathon be in person or virtual?

As of October 1st, the hackathon will be in person. If cases increase dramatically, the hackathon may be moved to a virtual platform. Our hackers’ and community’s safety are our biggest priority.
There may be a mix of virtual technical workshops.

What safety protocols are in place to protect the community?

University of Rochester COVID-19 protocols: https://www.rochester.edu/coronavirus-update/

Can I sign up with a team? What if I don’t have one?

Absolutely! Keeping in line with our previous years, you’ll be able to submit a project with three additional teammates!

If you don’t have a team though, we’ll have a team building channel in our Discord!

Can I start hacking before the event?

All projects must be made during the time frame of the event. Planning and gathering resources before the event are ok.
